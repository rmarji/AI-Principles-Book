## Bottom Line

Chapter 7 is fundamentally strong in its conceptual framework and writing quality. The Decision Quality Framework, cognitive bias coverage, and practical applications are well-executed and valuable. However, **three critical issues block publication readiness:**

**1. Excessive Length (BLOCKING):** At 691 lines, the chapter is 30% too long. It tries to cover too much ground—from basic research agents through advanced scenario planning, Devil's Advocate protocols, and decision memory systems. Readers will feel overwhelmed. The solution is clear: split into two chapters. Chapter 7A covers foundations (research agents, analysis agents, frameworks, biases) while Chapter 7B tackles advanced techniques (scenario planning, Devil's Advocate, protocols, decision memory). This split also creates opportunity to expand underdeveloped areas like ethics and implementation without further bloating a single chapter.

**2. Outdated Anchor Statistic (CRITICAL):** The first statistic readers encounter is from McKinsey June 2017—eight years old and predating the ChatGPT revolution. This immediately undermines credibility in a book about AI leadership in 2025. The entire chapter suffers from over-reliance on pre-2020 sources. While classic research (Simon 1955, Kahneman 2011) is acceptable for foundational concepts, research on AI capabilities must be current. Every claim about what AI agents "can do" needs validation from 2023-2025 research or experience.

**3. Missing Modern AI Coverage (HIGH PRIORITY):** The chapter reads as though written in 2020-2021, before ChatGPT, Claude, and modern agentic AI. There's no mention of extended thinking, Perplexity, multimodal analysis, RAG, or other capabilities that define 2025 AI decision support. The ethics section is cursory (43 lines) when 2025 demands depth on bias, accountability, transparency, and governance. Implementation guidance is absent—no discussion of costs, build/buy decisions, integration requirements, or training needs.

The good news: the underlying structure and writing are solid. The bad news: achieving 2025 publication readiness requires substantial work—not surface-level updates but meaningful rewriting, restructuring, and expansion in critical areas. Budget 30-40 hours for comprehensive revision including the chapter split, or 20-30 hours for aggressive editing if keeping as single chapter (not recommended). This is high-priority work that must be completed before publication.
