## Staying Current: A Recommended Routine

### Daily (5-10 minutes)

- Scan one AI newsletter
- Check LinkedIn for industry updates
- Review any AI tools you're actively using

### Weekly (1-2 hours)

- Read 1-2 in-depth articles or papers
- Listen to one AI podcast episode
- Experiment with a new AI feature or tool

### Monthly (2-4 hours)

- Read one AI-focused book or report
- Attend one virtual event or webinar
- Review and update your AI knowledge goals

### Quarterly (1 day)

- Take one short online course
- Conduct an AI landscape review
- Update your organization's AI strategy

## Building Your AI Learning Path

### For Executives

**Quarter 1:** Foundations
- Read *Co-Intelligence* or *Prediction Machines*
- Take AI for Business Leaders course
- Subscribe to 2-3 newsletters

**Quarter 2:** Strategy
- Read *Competing in the Age of AI*
- Attend one AI business conference
- Conduct organizational AI assessment

**Quarter 3:** Implementation
- Deep dive on one AI vendor's enterprise offering
- Pilot one AI agent use case
- Build internal AI governance framework

**Quarter 4:** Scaling
- Review State of AI Report
- Develop multi-year AI roadmap
- Plan team AI literacy initiatives

### For Managers

**Month 1-2:** Understanding
- Complete one foundational AI course
- Experiment with ChatGPT/Claude daily
- Read *Human + Machine*

**Month 3-4:** Application
- Identify AI opportunities in your area
- Pilot one workflow automation
- Build prompt templates for your team

**Month 5-6:** Leadership
- Train team on AI tools
- Establish team AI guidelines
- Measure and report on AI impact

## Resource Evaluation Criteria

When evaluating new AI resources, consider:

| Criterion | Questions to Ask |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Recency** | When was this published? Is it still relevant given AI's pace? |
| **Credibility** | What is the author's background? Are claims substantiated? |
| **Practicality** | Can I apply this to my work? Are there concrete examples? |
| **Balance** | Does it acknowledge limitations and risks, not just hype? |
| **Accessibility** | Is it appropriate for my current knowledge level? |
